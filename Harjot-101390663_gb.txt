George Brown College is a public college located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. The college was founded in 1967 and named after George Brown, a Scottish-born Canadian journalist, politician, and one of the Fathers of Confederation. George Brown College is one of the largest colleges in Ontario, with over 31,000 full-time students, including more than 3,200 international students from 130 countries.
The college offers a wide range of programs and courses, including certificates, diplomas, and degree programs in areas such as business, community services, culinary arts, design, engineering technologies, health sciences, hospitality and tourism, liberal arts and sciences, and preparatory studies. George Brown College is also known for its apprenticeship programs, which provide hands-on training in various skilled trades.
George Brown College has three main campuses in Toronto: St. James Campus, Waterfront Campus, and Casa Loma Campus. St. James Campus is the largest and oldest campus and is located in the heart of downtown Toronto. Waterfront Campus is located on Toronto's revitalized waterfront and offers programs in hospitality, culinary arts, and tourism. Casa Loma Campus is situated in a historic castle and offers programs in health sciences and community services.

George Brown College has a strong focus on applied research and innovation, partnering with industry and community organizations to provide students with real-world learning opportunities. The college also has a number of international partnerships and exchange programs, allowing students to study abroad and gain valuable international experience.

Overall, George Brown College is a respected institution known for its practical, career-focused programs and its commitment to innovation and community engagement.
